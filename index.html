Investment Advisor Assessment


Could Your Investment Advisor Be Cheating You?


Studies have shown that overall, people who use an investment advisor do better financially than those who do not. However, many people give their complete trust to their investment advisor without really know if that person is trustworthy and is doing what's best for them. This is very worrisome because if it turns out the investment advisor is not trustworthy, you could lose most or all of what you have.


If you don't have a good understanding of investing and you rely on an advisor for advice or management, this assessment is meant for you.


This assessment is intended to identify whether certain behaviors frequently associated with losses due to unacceptable or improper behavior by an investment advisor exist in your investment advisory relationship. It does not guarantee it will identify all risks that might cause you a loss, whether caused by your investment advisor or not; nor does the presence of some risk factors necessarily mean you should drop your advisor. Where risks are identified, you would be prudent to examine them more closely and take steps to reduce your exposure.


On the other hand, if your assessment does not identify high levels of risk, it doesn't mean you don't have any risk or that you won't have future losses. This assessment doesn't cover every untrustworthy behavior or risk factor that might exist in your situation. In addition, there are many other areas where your finances can be at risk without any unacceptable or improper behavior by your investment advisor.


Please answer all of the following questions. The questions are about your investment advisor, not about your knowledge of investing. Your answers should be based on your understanding of the questions and best recollection.


When you complete the Assessment and submit your answers, you will receive an onscreen report identifying your risk scores by category and an interpretation of that score. If you have high risk scores in one or more areas, you should seriously consider undertaking a detailed review of your investment portfolio.
